AI writes and ships code faster than humans can review it. We think the next security layer has to **attack continuously, prove impact with evidence instead of scores, and hand engineers — and AI agents — a reproducible fix.**

Most AI-reliability tooling measures prompts, traces, and outputs. We care about whether a capable system can actually be **broken, exploited, or pushed outside its intended bounds** — and whether you can reproduce it.

## What we build

- 🤖 &nbsp;**pwnkit** — our autonomous hacking engine. Research agents find vulnerabilities; independent verify agents re-exploit each one and kill anything they can't reproduce. Closed source. → [0sec.ai/pwnkit](https://0sec.ai/pwnkit)
- ☁️ &nbsp;**0cloud** — the managed product around pwnkit: scoped targets, recurring runs, exploit-backed evidence, and triage workflows. → [0sec.ai](https://0sec.ai)
- 🦊 &nbsp;**[foxguard](https://github.com/0sec-labs/foxguard)** — open-source, linter-fast security scanning across 12 languages: rules, taint tracking, secrets, dependency and post-quantum crypto audits. Our open trust signal.
- 🔀 &nbsp;**[opensoar](https://github.com/opensoar-hq/opensoar-core)** — open-source SOAR: routing validated failures into real operator response and orchestration workflows.
- 🔬 &nbsp;**[noeris](https://github.com/0sec-labs/noeris)** — our research OS for autonomous empirical discovery, currently led by a flagship GPU kernel-optimization track.

## How we think about it

- ⚔️ &nbsp;attacks and working exploits, not happy-path evals
- 🧾 &nbsp;evidence-backed findings, not vibe-based scores
- 🛡️ &nbsp;prevention and response, not detection alone

## Why now

AI agents are being shipped into real workflows faster than teams can verify them. The bottleneck is no longer generation — it's **trust**. 0sec Labs exists to close that gap.
